# Scientific Limits

WAI-R0 v0.6 is an experiment-control platform, not evidence that its candidate architectures are superior.

## What results can establish

A well-controlled run can establish that, under the declared local conditions, a candidate:

- is numerically correct;
- changes measured cache allocation or latency;
- improves or harms a generated held-out task metric;
- remains stable across declared seeds;
- satisfies or fails a preregistered threshold.

## What results cannot establish

The same run cannot establish:

- human-like reasoning;
- frontier-model capability;
- scale transfer;
- safety or reliability in deployment;
- superiority on natural language without natural-language evaluation;
- causality when candidate/control matching is invalid;
- generality beyond the tested distributions.

## Specific caveats

- MLA-lite is a local compressed-cache experiment, not DeepSeek MLA.
- Recurrent latent updates are not called thoughts.
- Symbolic solver results remain symbolic or hybrid evidence.
- A single blended score is not a valid substitute for mandatory gates.
- Generated tasks can be overfit through generator-aware tuning.
- Near-duplicate contamination may survive exact hashing.
- CPU performance rankings may reverse on GPU.
- Eight-gigabyte hardware supports screening experiments, not 7B pretraining.
